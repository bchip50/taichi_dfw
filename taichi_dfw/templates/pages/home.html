Introduction

Tai Chi Chuan is a collection of Chinese exercises that have developed over the centuries. Tai Chi is an internal martial art (Wudong). This means that it is mostly focused on physical health and balance. Other martial arts try to build the body in a way to achieve physical mastery over others. Tai Chi is more concerned with individual mastery over yourself.

Objectives for Tai Chi practice:
  • Balance is improved by the various movements that strengthen the muscles. Some moves require that we stand on one foot while moving. This improves the sense of balance since the movements are very slow.
  • Flexability improves as the movements are gradualy expanded and practiced. The variety of movements streach and strengthen the body.
  • Breath is controlled as the movements are practiced. Each Tai Chi movement has a definite breathing pattern. The slow movements prompt slower, more deliberate breathing.
  • Propreception is the sense of self-movement and body position. This sense is developed as you practice TaiChi.

This Site

Tai Chi classes and clubs exist all over the Metroplex. This site is your guide to finding those classes and clubs. The site is divided into the various styles of Tai Chi. These are listed in the menu. Just choose the style you want. The style page will give you a brief introduction to the style along with a list of available classes and clubs. Click on the class or club to get details and sign up. Note that some sessions have a cost, although most of the clubs are free.

Registration

You will need to create an account on this web site to sign up for classes or view any of the secured materials. There is no charge for this site. You will not be asked for a credit card. The membership list will only be used by the instructors and hosts who create and manage the membership of the classes or clubs.

Instructors or hosts should contact the site administrator to be designated as an class or club manager. Note that managers can designate other members as instructors, also. Instructors will be responsible for setting up the classes or clubs that the wish to list on the site. Instructors will also be responsible for the membership list and notifications of meetings. Instructors can also create links to resources that will be useful to the members. Note that photos and videos can be associated with a class or club, but they will need to be stored in another site that the available via a shared link - like from youtube.
